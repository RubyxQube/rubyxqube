# Discovery Call Guide

This is a 15-minute call. You are not closing a deal on this call.
The goal is simple: understand their situation well enough to know which package fits, then get a clear next step before you hang up.

Listen 70%. Talk 30%.

---

## Before the Call (5 min prep)

Pull up their current site and the researcher profile. Know:
- What they do and where they serve
- What's broken about their current online presence
- Which package is probably the right fit before the call starts
- One specific thing to mention that shows you actually looked at them

---

## The Opening (don't pitch yet)

Keep it loose. They said yes to the call because the demo impressed them.
You don't need to re-sell it.

```
"Hey [Name], Boyd here - thanks for making time. 
So before I talk about anything, I just want to understand 
your situation a bit. Tell me what's going on with your 
business right now - how are you getting most of your leads?"
```

Then shut up and listen.

---

## The Questions

Work through these naturally, not as a checklist. You don't need all of them.

**On their current situation:**
- "How are people finding you right now?"
- "Are you happy with the volume of leads you're getting?"
- "What does your current website do for you - is it bringing in calls?"

**On pain:**
- "What's the biggest frustration with how things are set up online?"
- "Have you tried anything to fix it before?"

**On timing:**
- "Is this something you're looking to move on soon or are you just exploring?"

**On budget (if they haven't mentioned it):**
- "Just so I'm not wasting your time - do you have a rough budget in mind for something like this?"

---

## Matching the Package

Based on what you heard, steer toward the right fit. Don't offer all three options at once - it creates confusion. Lead with one.

**Lead with Launch if:**
- They just need a site, no existing one or very outdated
- Budget is tight or they mentioned "I just want something basic"
- They didn't mention lead volume as a big concern

**Lead with Autopilot if:**
- They mentioned missing calls or leads going cold
- They work jobs during the day and can't answer the phone
- They seemed impressed by the chatbot on the demo

**Lead with Momentum if:**
- They mentioned competitors outranking them on Google
- They have reviews but aren't showing up locally
- They want ongoing growth, not just a one-time site

---

## Presenting the Package

Don't read them a feature list. Connect it to what they told you.

```
"Based on what you said about missing calls while you're on a job - 
I think Autopilot is the right fit for you. It's a full site plus 
an AI receptionist that answers questions and captures leads 24/7. 
So when someone finds you at 9pm, they get a response instead of 
bouncing to a competitor."
```

Then give the price directly. Don't bury it.

```
"It's $3,000 upfront and $399 a month. The monthly covers the AI, 
updates, and a monthly report so you can see how it's performing."
```

---

## Handling the Common Objections

**"That's more than I was expecting."**

```
"I get that. What were you thinking? Just want to make sure 
I'm not pitching you the wrong thing."
```

Then actually listen. If they're genuinely a Launch fit, offer that instead.

**"I need to think about it."**

```
"Of course. What would help you make the decision - 
is there something specific you'd want to know more about?"
```

If they have no specific concern, they're not sold yet. Ask what's holding them back.

**"I had a bad experience with a web designer before."**

```
"What happened?"
```

Let them vent. Don't defend the industry. Then:

```
"That's exactly why I do things differently - you see the work 
before you pay anything. The demo is already built. You're not 
paying for a promise."
```

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**

Don't discount unless you're dropping scope. Offer Launch if Autopilot is too much.

```
"I don't discount, but I can adjust what's included. 
The Launch package is $2,500 one-time, no monthly. 
That gets you the full site without the AI receptionist."
```

---

## Getting the Next Step

Before you hang up, get something concrete. Not "I'll think about it and get back to you."

Options:
- "Want me to send over a simple agreement so you can look it over?"
- "Should we set a follow-up call for next week once you've had time to think?"
- "If you're ready to move, I just need a 50% deposit to get started."

If they're not ready to commit, that's fine. Get a specific follow-up date.

```
"No rush at all. How about I check back in with you on [specific day]?"
```

---

## After the Call

Send a follow-up email within 30 minutes while it's fresh. Keep it short:

```
Hey [Name],

Good talking to you. To recap - [one sentence summary of what they need 
and which package fits].

[If they're close to deciding:]
I'll send over the agreement today. Just needs a signature and 50% to kick things off.

[If they need time:]
I'll follow up [specific day]. In the meantime, feel free to reach out with any questions.

Boyd
(208) 970-8624
```

---

## What a Win Looks Like

Not every call ends with a yes. A win is any of these:
- They sign or say yes on the call
- They ask for the agreement
- You have a specific follow-up date they committed to

A call where they say "sounds good, I'll think about it" with no follow-up date is not a win. Push gently for something concrete before ending.
