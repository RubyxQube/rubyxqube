# RubyxQube — Lead Generation Playbook

> How to consistently generate new client leads — both warm and cold.  
> The goal: never run dry. Always have 3–5 conversations in progress.

---

## The Core Funnel

```
Attention → Interest → Audit → Proposal → Close
```

- **Attention:** They see us (Google, referral, social, cold outreach)
- **Interest:** They visit rubyxqube.com, chat with our bot, or respond to outreach
- **Audit:** Free 15-min audit call (Calendly) — this is where deals are won
- **Proposal:** Send same day, follow up 24–48 hrs later
- **Close:** Signed contract + 50% deposit = client

---

## Tier 1 — Warm Network (Start Here)

These close fastest and cost nothing. Do this first.

### Who to contact
- Friends/family who own or manage a local business
- Bastion MSP clients and contacts — many are small business owners
- Past coworkers, classmates, neighbors
- People you know in trades (plumbers, contractors, HVAC, landscapers)

### Script (text or DM)
> "Hey [Name] — I started a web design agency called RubyxQube. We build websites and AI chatbots for local service businesses. Do you know anyone who owns a small business with a bad website or no website at all? I'd love an intro, or if it's you — happy to take a look for free."

- Low pressure, ask for referrals not just direct business
- Offer the free audit as the next step, not a sales pitch

### Founding Client Pricing
For your first 3 clients: offer 50% off the setup fee in exchange for:
- A written testimonial
- Permission to use their site as a portfolio piece
- A Google review

This removes the price objection and gets you real case studies fast.

## Tier 2 — Cold Outreach (Systematic)

### How to find leads
Search Google Maps for service businesses in the Treasure Valley:
- `"plumber Boise"` — look for ones with no website or website linked in GBP
- `"landscaper Meridian Idaho"`
- `"house cleaning Nampa"`
- `"roofing contractor Caldwell"`
- `"electrician Eagle Idaho"`

**Red flags = opportunity:**
- GBP listing says "No website"
- Website linked but goes to a Wix/Squarespace free-tier page from 2015
- Site loads slow, looks broken on mobile, no clear phone number visible
- No chatbot, no contact form, just a phone number

### Cold outreach email template
**Subject:** Quick question about [Business Name]'s website

> Hi [Name],
>
> I found [Business Name] on Google while looking for [service] in [city] — great reviews.
>
> I noticed your website [doesn't load well on mobile / is hard to find / doesn't have a way to get quotes online]. Local businesses lose real customers to that every day.
>
> I build websites + AI receptionists for service businesses in the Treasure Valley. I'd love to record a free 5-minute video showing exactly what I'd fix — no pitch, just honest feedback.
>
> Want me to send it over?
>
> Boyd Querubin  
> RubyxQube | rubyxqube.com  
> (208) [your number]

### Follow-up cadence
- Day 1: Send email
- Day 3: Follow up ("Just wanted to make sure this didn't get buried…")
- Day 7: Final follow up, then move on
- No more than 3 touches — respect their time

### Cold DM (Facebook / Instagram)
Same message, shorter. Local business Facebook pages often check their messages.

## Tier 3 — Local Community (Organic)

### Facebook Groups
Join these and participate genuinely. Post value, not ads.
- "Treasure Valley Small Business Network"
- "Boise Entrepreneurs"
- "Boise/Meridian/Nampa community groups"
- Any local trades or contractor groups

When someone posts "does anyone know a good web designer?" — that's your moment. Reply fast.

**Post ideas (value, not ads):**
- "5 things I check on every Boise business website" (educational)
- "Here's a site we just launched for a local stonework company" (case study)
- "We do free website audits for local businesses — anyone want one?" (direct offer)

### Nextdoor
Create a business account. Service business owners are on Nextdoor too.

### Google Business Profile
- Set up GBP for RubyxQube (if not done)
- Category: "Website Designer" + "Marketing Agency"
- Ask every satisfied client for a 5-star Google review
- Post updates monthly (completed projects, tips)

## Tier 4 — Strategic Partnerships

### Who to partner with
These professionals already serve small business owners and can refer constantly:
- **Business accountants / CPAs** — their clients always need better online presence
- **Commercial real estate agents** — new businesses opening locations
- **Insurance agents** — small business clients
- **IT consultants** — Bastion MSP is already this for you

### Partnership deal
> "I'll refer clients to you when they need [their service]. In return, when you have a client who needs a website or AI chatbot, send them my way. No commission needed — just referrals."

Formal referral: offer $200–300 per closed client referral once you can afford it.

## Tier 5 — Paid (Phase 2+)

Don't spend money on ads until you have:
- A proven close rate from free channels
- At least 2–3 client case studies
- A clean, fast landing page optimized for conversions (the `/audit` page)

### Google Ads
- Target: `"web design Boise"`, `"website designer Treasure Valley"`, `"small business website Idaho"`
- Budget: start at $300/mo, measure cost-per-lead
- Landing page: `/audit` — one CTA, one form, no distractions

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram) Ads
- Retargeting: anyone who visited rubyxqube.com but didn't convert
- Cold: target local business owners in Ada/Canyon County
- Creative: before/after site screenshots, chatbot demo video

## The Free Audit as a Sales Tool

This is the most powerful thing in the funnel. Use it everywhere.

**What it is:** A free 5–15 min video (Loom) or live call where you:
1. Show their current site's problems (mobile view, slow load, missing CTA)
2. Show a competitor who's doing it better
3. Explain what it's costing them in missed leads
4. Offer the solution (your package)

**Where to offer it:**
- Homepage CTA → `/audit` page
- Cold outreach emails
- Facebook group replies
- GBP listing description

**The close rate is high** because you're giving real value upfront. People feel obligated to at least hear the proposal.

## Tracking Leads

Use the Notion CRM board with these stages:

```
Prospect → Contacted → Audit Scheduled → Proposal Sent → Negotiating → Closed → Lost
```

For every lead, track:
- Business name + owner name
- How they came in (cold outreach, referral, website, etc.)
- Date of first contact
- Last touchpoint
- Next action + date

Review this board every Monday. If something has been sitting in "Contacted" for 2 weeks with no response, move it to Lost and move on.
