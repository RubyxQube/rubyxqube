## Service Agreement (Contract)

You need a signed contract before doing any paid work. No exceptions.

### Where to get a template
- **Bonsai** (bonsai.io) — freelance-specific, has web design templates, free tier available
- **Docracy** (docracy.com) — free legal templates
- **HelloSign / Docusign** — for e-signatures once you have a template

### What must be in every contract

**Scope of Work**
- Exactly what you're delivering (pages, features, chatbot, revisions)
- What is NOT included (photography, copywriting, SEO content, ecommerce)
- Number of revision rounds (2 rounds recommended — unlimited revisions = scope creep)

**Timeline**
- Project start date (after signed contract + deposit received)
- Estimated launch date
- "Timeline subject to client responsiveness" — protects you if they ghost you

**Payment Terms**
- 50% deposit to start (non-refundable after work begins)
- 50% due on launch day before DNS cutover
- Monthly retainer billed on the 1st of each month
- Late fee: 1.5% per month on overdue balances
- Accepted methods: bank transfer, Zelle, card via Wave

**Intellectual Property**
- Client owns all final deliverables **upon receipt of final payment**
- Before final payment: RubyxQube retains ownership
- You retain the right to show the work in your portfolio (unless client objects in writing)

**Hosting Continuity**
- If monthly retainer is not paid within 30 days: service paused (site may go offline)
- After 60 days of non-payment: contract terminated, client receives all files
- Client is responsible for their own domain registration and renewal

**Termination**
- Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice
- Client receives all files and assets upon final payment
- No refund of setup fee if client cancels mid-build after work has begun

**Liability**
- RubyxQube is not liable for business losses, lost revenue, or damages from website downtime
- Client is responsible for the accuracy of all content they provide (services, pricing, claims)
- Client indemnifies RubyxQube against claims arising from their content

## Privacy Policy

✅ Already live on rubyxqube.com at `/privacy`

Every client site needs its own privacy policy too. Template it once, customize per client.

What it must cover:
- What data is collected (name, email, phone via contact form + chatbot)
- How it's used (to respond to inquiries)
- Who it's shared with (Anthropic API for chatbot processing, Twilio for SMS)
- Cookie/analytics disclosure (GA4)
- Contact info for data requests

## Idaho-Specific Notes

- **No sales tax on digital services in Idaho** — you don't charge sales tax on web design services. ✅
- **Business registration:** LLC registered with Idaho SOS — you're good.
- **EIN:** Get one from IRS.gov (free, takes 5 minutes online). Needed for: business bank account, Wave, paying contractors, 1099s.
- **Registered Agent:** Idaho requires a registered agent with an Idaho address. If you used a registered agent service, note their address — don't publish your home address.

## Contracts for Subcontractors / Freelancers

When you eventually hire a contractor (designer, copywriter, VA):

**Must have:**
- Independent Contractor Agreement (not employee agreement)
- States they are not an employee — you don't withhold taxes
- IP assignment clause — work they do for you belongs to RubyxQube
- NDA clause — they can't share client info or poach your clients
- Issue a 1099-NEC if you pay them more than $600 in a calendar year
