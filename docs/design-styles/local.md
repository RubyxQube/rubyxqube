# The Local — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Restaurant · Café · Salon · Bakery · Boutique · Florist · Gift Shop · Local Retail

---

## Core Philosophy

Warmth and belonging. People choose local businesses because of how they make them feel. The site should feel like walking in for the first time — inviting, personal, and a little delightful. Reviews, photos, and story are the engine.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#fef9f0`    | Warm cream — cozy, not clinical |
| Navbar     | `#2d5016`    | Deep green — grounded, natural |
| Hero bg    | `#fef9f0`    | Same as body or hero image overlay |
| Accent     | `#2d5016`    | Forest green — buttons, badges |
| Text       | `#1c1917`    | Near-black warm tone |
| Muted      | `#a8a29e`    | Warm gray for secondary content |
| Cards      | `#fef3c7`    | Warm amber-yellow lift |

Client palette may vary — this works in terracotta, sage green, dusty rose, or navy depending on the business.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Friendly weight (600–700). Can use a slightly softer font.
- **Hero H1:** 42–52px. Welcoming, not aggressive. "Fresh-Baked Every Morning." "Your neighborhood salon."
- **Body:** 16px, 1.7 line-height. Reads conversationally.
- **Accent text:** Can use a display or script font sparingly for the business name or pull quote — but keep it legible.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Dark green or branded color background
- Logo center (or left) — this style often looks great with a centered logo
- Links: Menu/Services | About | Reviews | Contact
- CTA: "Reserve a Table" / "Book Appointment" / "Order Online" depending on business

### 2. Hero Section
- Full-width, high-quality lifestyle photo (food, storefront, team smiling)
- If no great photo: warm gradient or soft texture overlay on cream background
- **H1:** Warm and inviting. Business name or tagline.
- **Subhead:** "Handcrafted pastries in downtown Boise. Open 7am–3pm."
- **CTAs:** "See Our Menu" / "Book a Table" — rounded buttons feel right here

### 3. Featured Item or Signature Section
- Show off the thing they're known for
- Restaurant: 3 featured dishes with photo + name + price
- Salon: 3–4 signature services with photo + name
- Boutique: 3 product highlights
- Big beautiful images, minimal text — let the product speak

### 4. Story / About Teaser
- 2-column: photo of owner or interior left, short story right
- 3–5 sentences. Personal. "My grandmother taught me to bake in this very kitchen…"
- Link: "Read our full story →"

### 5. Reviews Section (CRITICAL for local businesses)
- 4–6 Google reviews
- Show star rating, reviewer name, quote
- Include "See all [X] reviews on Google →" link
- This section converts browsers into visitors

### 6. Menu / Services
- Restaurant: Embedded PDF or styled menu section with categories
- Salon: Service list with prices in a clean grid
- Boutique: Product category tiles or Instagram grid
- Keep it scannable — long menus should link to a dedicated page

### 7. Hours + Location
- Google Maps embed
- Big, clear hours (Mon–Fri: 8am–6pm / Sat: 9am–4pm / Closed Sun)
- Address with tap-to-navigate link on mobile
- Parking notes if relevant

### 8. Instagram / Social Grid (optional)
- Embedded Instagram feed (4–6 posts)
- "Follow us @[handle]" CTA
- Works great for food, boutique, salon — anything visual

### 9. Email Sign-up (optional)
- "Join our list for specials and new arrivals"
- Single field: email + button
- Integrate with Mailchimp or just a simple form

### 10. Footer
- Warm, friendly — not corporate
- Business name, address, phone, email, hours
- Social links (Instagram, Facebook typically)
- "Made with ❤ in [City]" — optional personal touch

---

## Mobile Rules
- Photos must be gorgeous on mobile — use responsive srcset or good object-fit
- Hours and address always near the top on mobile (people search on their phone)
- Menu must be scrollable without zooming
- Booking/reservation CTA should be tappable and prominent

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name, owner name, story/origin
- [ ] 8–12 high-quality photos (food, storefront, interior, team)
- [ ] Menu / service list with prices (or "market price" / "starting at")
- [ ] Hours and address
- [ ] 4–6 Google reviews (or link to their Google page)
- [ ] Reservation/booking link or phone for reservations
- [ ] Social media handles (especially Instagram)
- [ ] Email list platform if they have one

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't make it look corporate — warmth is the whole point
- Don't use stock photos — real photos of the actual business convert far better
- Don't bury the hours and location — this is the #1 thing mobile users want
- Don't skip reviews — local business without visible reviews feels untrustworthy
- Don't make the menu a PDF nightmare on mobile — style it or link a clean version
