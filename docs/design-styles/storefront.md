# The Storefront — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Online Shop · Boutique · Handmade Goods · Specialty Food · Gift Shop · Craft Brewery · Small-Batch Producer · Artist Selling Work

---

## Core Philosophy

The product IS the site. Every design decision exists to make the product look amazing and remove friction between browsing and buying. Clean layout, high-contrast product photography, and a clear path to purchase. Whether they're selling in-store, online, or both — the product comes first.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#ffffff`    | White — product photos need a clean stage |
| Navbar     | `#18181b`    | Near-black — sharp contrast with white page |
| Hero bg    | `#f4f4f5`    | Very light gray — neutral, photographic |
| Accent     | `#7c3aed`    | Purple — creative, distinctive, memorable |
| Text       | `#18181b`    | Deep black for maximum readability |
| Muted      | `#71717a`    | Mid-gray for secondary text |
| Cards      | `#fafafa`    | Off-white — subtle product card lift |

Client palette may use brand colors: dusty rose for a boutique, kraft/cream for handmade goods, black + gold for premium shop, amber for brewery. The key: high contrast between background and product photos.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Bold (700) for product names and sections. Clean, modern.
- **Hero H1:** 48–60px. Brand-first. The shop name or tagline as the hero. "Handcrafted in Boise." / "The gift shop for people who hate gift shops."
- **Product names:** 16–18px, bold, legible. Price in accent color or standard black.
- **Body:** 16px, 1.6 line-height. Easy to scan.
- **Consider:** A slightly playful or artisan-feeling font for the brand mark or hero heading — keeps it human without losing legibility.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Dark bar, white text
- Logo left (or brand wordmark)
- Links: Shop | Collections | About | Contact
- Right: Search icon + Cart (if e-commerce) OR "Visit Us" for physical stores
- CTA: "Shop Now" or "Order Online" — accent color button

### 2. Hero Section
- The single most important visual investment
- Full-width hero: best product photo or lifestyle shot
- Centered or left-aligned text overlay (or side-by-side layout)
- **H1:** Brand tagline or seasonal/current featured product
- **Subhead:** 1 line — what they sell + why it matters
- **CTAs:** "Shop Now" + "See Our Story" (secondary)
- Optional: sale/promo badge — "Free shipping over $50" or "New arrivals weekly"

### 3. Featured Products / Best Sellers
- Grid of 3–6 products
- Each card: product photo + name + price + "Add to Cart" or "Shop →"
- Photos must be consistent (same background, same lighting, same crop ratio)
- "View All →" link to full shop

### 4. Collections / Categories
- If they sell multiple categories, surface them here
- 3–4 category tiles with cover photo + "Shop [Category] →"
- Examples: "Candles | Ceramics | Apparel | Home Goods"

### 5. Brand Story / About Section
- "Made by hand. Made with purpose." — short, editorial tone
- 2-column: product lifestyle photo left, story paragraph right
- "We started [Business] because..." — 3–4 sentences max
- Link: "Our full story →"

### 6. Featured Product Spotlight
- One highlighted product with larger format
- Photo on the left (or full-bleed background), description + buy button right
- Used for a signature product, seasonal item, or gift bundle
- "This month's favorite" / "Our best seller" / "The perfect gift"

### 7. Customer Reviews / UGC
- 4–6 reviews with star ratings
- Photos of customers with the product if available (UGC = user-generated content)
- "See all reviews →"
- For handmade/boutique: Etsy reviews, Google reviews, or direct testimonials

### 8. Instagram / Social Grid
- "Follow along @[handle]" — horizontal 6-photo Instagram grid
- Especially powerful for visual product categories (ceramics, clothing, food)
- Embeds real social proof and lifestyle context

### 9. Email Sign-Up / Newsletter
- "Join our list for new arrivals and exclusive offers"
- 1 field: email + button
- Optional lead magnet: "Get 10% off your first order"

### 10. Visit Us / In-Store Info (if physical location)
- Address, hours, parking
- Google Maps embed
- "Can't make it in? Shop online →"

### 11. Footer
- Logo, social links, nav links, contact
- Return/exchange policy link (critical for e-commerce trust)
- "Handmade in [City, State]" — provenance builds product value

## E-commerce vs. Brochure Mode

**If they have an online shop (Shopify, WooCommerce, Etsy):**
- Link product cards directly to the shop platform
- "Shop Now" CTA goes to their store
- Don't try to rebuild Shopify — link to it

**If they're local-only / no online shop:**
- Product cards show items but CTA is "Visit Us" or "Order by Phone"
- Keep a simple order form or contact form for custom orders
- Gallery mode works great — show products, drive foot traffic

---

## Mobile Rules
- Product grid: 2 columns on mobile minimum (never 1 — shoppers expect to browse)
- Product photos: must not crop badly — use consistent aspect ratios (square or 4:5)
- "Add to Cart" / "Shop Now" always above the fold on mobile
- Cart icon must be tappable and visible at all times (if e-commerce)

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name, tagline, brand story
- [ ] 12–25 product photos (consistent lighting and background)
- [ ] Product names and prices (or "call for pricing" for custom)
- [ ] Collections / categories (if they have them)
- [ ] 4–6 customer reviews
- [ ] Instagram handle for social grid
- [ ] Online store URL (Shopify/Etsy/WooCommerce) if applicable
- [ ] Physical store address and hours if applicable
- [ ] Shipping / return policy

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use bad product photos — no amount of CSS fixes dark, blurry product shots
- Don't list every product on the homepage — curate 3–6 best sellers and link to the shop
- Don't make the purchase path more than 2 clicks
- Don't skip the brand story — product buyers want to know the person behind the product
- Don't use the same accent color as the product photos — it'll clash
