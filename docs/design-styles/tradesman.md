# The Tradesman — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Plumbing · HVAC · Electrical · Roofing · Landscaping · Concrete · Any trade that competes on trust + urgency

---

## Core Philosophy

Built to convert. Tradesman sites have one job: make someone pick up the phone or fill out the form. Every design decision serves that goal. This is not a portfolio — it's a lead machine.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#111827`    | Dark slate — serious and high-contrast |
| Navbar     | `#0f172a`    | Deeper than body — grounds the top |
| Hero bg    | `#111827`    | Same as body — continuous feel |
| Accent     | `#f59e0b`    | Amber — high-visibility CTAs, badges, icons |
| Text       | `#ffffff`    | Full white on dark bg |
| Muted      | `#6b7280`    | Secondary text, fine print |
| Cards      | `#1f2937`    | Lifted just above background |

Client colors may override. Keep high contrast. Amber is the default — blue or red are also strong alternatives for this style.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Heavy (700–800). Large. No room for subtlety.
- **Hero H1:** 48–60px. Short punchy phrase. "Boise's #1 Plumber." "Fast HVAC Repair."
- **Body:** 16–17px, readable, white/muted.
- **Don't** use thin weights or decorative fonts. Plus Jakarta Sans works. Montserrat or Oswald also solid.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Dark background, amber CTA button on the right: **"Call Now"** or **"Get a Free Quote"**
- Logo left, nav links center (optional — can skip links entirely for minimal look)
- Phone number visible in nav on desktop

### 2. Hero Section
- Dark bg, full-width
- Left-aligned or centered text
- **H1:** Short and direct. "Fast, Reliable [Service] in [City]."
- **Subhead:** 1–2 sentences max. "Licensed & insured. 24/7 emergency service."
- **CTAs:** Primary = phone number button. Secondary = "Get a free estimate"
- Optional: badge/chip — "⚡ Same-Day Service" or "✓ Licensed & Insured"
- Hero image or video on the right (half-width on desktop), or subtle grid/texture bg

### 3. Trust Bar
- Full-width, slightly darker or same as hero
- 3–5 trust signals in a row: icons + short phrases
  - ✓ Licensed & Insured  |  ⭐ 4.9 Stars  |  📞 24/7 Emergency  |  🏠 Serving [City] Since [Year]

### 4. Services Section
- Grid of service cards (3 columns desktop, 1–2 mobile)
- Each card: icon, service name, 1-line description
- Cards on `#1f2937` surface with amber accent icon
- Link each card to service detail (or just scroll-to-contact if no subpages)

### 5. Social Proof
- **Reviews prominently placed above the fold on scroll**
- 3–5 Google review cards with star rating, customer name, short quote
- Option: Google review badge or "4.9 stars — 87 reviews on Google"

### 6. About / Why Us
- 2-column: text left, photo right (owner on jobsite is gold)
- Keep it short: 2–3 sentences about the business, then a quick list of differentiators
- Feels personal but stays professional

### 7. Service Area Map or Text List
- "Serving: Boise, Meridian, Nampa, Eagle, Kuna, Star"
- Optional: embedded Google Map with service radius

### 8. Final CTA Section
- High-contrast full-width band — amber background or dark with amber border
- Big: "Ready to schedule?"
- Phone button + short form (name, phone, message)
- Don't make the form long. 3 fields max to start.

### 9. Footer
- Dark, simple
- NAP: Business name, address (if applicable), phone, email
- Links: Home, Services, About, Privacy
- "Serving [City] & surrounding areas"

---

## Mobile Rules
- Phone number must be tap-to-call everywhere it appears
- CTA button stays sticky on mobile or floats near bottom
- Cards stack vertically (1 column)
- No horizontal scroll anywhere

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name + owner first name
- [ ] Phone number (primary) + email
- [ ] Services list (what they offer + rough pricing if comfortable)
- [ ] Service area (cities or zip codes)
- [ ] Years in business
- [ ] License number (if they want to display)
- [ ] 3–5 Google reviews (copy/paste)
- [ ] Photo of owner or team on jobsite
- [ ] Logo (or we build one)
- [ ] Emergency/24-7 service? (yes/no)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't make the hero text too long — trades clients skim, then call
- Don't bury the phone number — it belongs in the nav AND the hero AND the footer
- Don't use light backgrounds — dark builds trust and authority for trades
- Don't use script or decorative fonts — they feel untrustworthy for this audience
