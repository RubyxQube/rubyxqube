# The Outdoors — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Tree Service · Fencing · Hunting Guide · Farming · Outdoor Recreation · Excavation · Irrigation · Wildlife Management

---

## Core Philosophy

Grounded, earned, and real. This audience respects experience and doesn't trust anything that looks too slick. The design should feel as solid as the work — earthy, confident, and no-nonsense. Show the work. Show the land. Prove they know what they're doing.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#f5f0e0`    | Warm parchment — earthy and grounded |
| Navbar     | `#1a3a1a`    | Deep forest green — authority |
| Hero bg    | `#f5f0e0`    | Same as body, or hero image |
| Accent     | `#2d6a2d`    | Forest green — natural CTAs |
| Text       | `#1c1917`    | Dark warm-black |
| Muted      | `#78716c`    | Earthy gray-brown for secondary |
| Cards      | `#e8e0cc`    | Slightly darker parchment |

Client palette may lean darker (hunting: deep camo tones, dark brown) or warmer (farming: golden wheat tones). Avoid anything that looks "tech" or "Silicon Valley."

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Bold and direct (700). No-nonsense headings.
- **Hero H1:** 44–56px. Confident and plain-spoken. "Boise's Most Trusted Tree Service." "Serving Idaho Hunters Since 1998."
- **Body:** 16px, 1.7 line-height. Plain, clear language. No jargon.
- **Avoid decorative fonts** — this audience is skeptical of anything that looks fancy or city-slicker.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Dark forest green, white text
- Logo left — ideally a mark that feels handcrafted or badge-like
- Minimal links: Services | About | Gallery | Contact
- CTA: "Call for a Free Quote" — green or accent button

### 2. Hero Section
- Strong hero image — wide-angle site/field/crew photo (NOT stock)
- If no photo: dark green or parchment with subtle texture overlay
- **H1:** Direct and regional. "Idaho's Premier Tree Service." "Treasure Valley Irrigation Experts."
- **Subhead:** What they do, where they do it, how long they've done it.
- **CTAs:** "Get a Free Estimate" + phone number as secondary

### 3. Trust Bar
- 4–5 signals in a horizontal row
- ✓ Fully Insured | 📅 [X] Years Experience | 📍 Serving [Region] | ⭐ [X] 5-Star Reviews | 🌲 [Fun Stat]

### 4. Services Section
- Grid of service cards — 3 columns desktop, 1 mobile
- Each: service name, 2-line description, optional photo
- Use real photos if available — even phone camera quality beats stock here
- Pricing: "Free estimates" is usually the right call for this style

### 5. Project Gallery / Work Portfolio
- Photo grid — 3–4 wide or masonry
- Before/after pairs work especially well (tree removal, fencing, excavation)
- Captions: location + job type. "Eagle, ID — 80-ft pine removal"
- This section is the #1 trust-builder for this style

### 6. About Section
- Owner story — how long, why they started, ties to the land/region
- Photo of owner/crew in the field (not a posed office photo)
- "We've been doing this work in [Region] since [Year]. We're not a call center — we're your neighbors."
- Credentials: ISA certified, licensed, insured, bonded

### 7. Service Area
- Map or city list: "Boise · Meridian · Nampa · Eagle · Middleton · Caldwell"
- Simple statement: "We travel up to [X] miles from downtown Boise"

### 8. Reviews
- 4–5 reviews, real names, short and direct quotes
- This audience reads reviews differently — they're looking for reliability, not charm

### 9. CTA / Contact
- Simple form: Name + Phone + Job Description
- "Or call us directly: (xxx) xxx-xxxx"
- Turnaround promise if they have one: "We return all calls within 2 hours."

### 10. Footer
- Dark green footer
- Business name, phone, license/insurance number
- "Locally owned. Serving [Region] since [Year]."

---

## Mobile Rules
- Hero image must scale without cropping the subject
- Gallery must work as a scrollable grid or full-screen tap-to-expand
- Phone CTA always tap-to-call
- Form stays simple — name, phone, brief job description (3 fields max)

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name, owner name, years in business
- [ ] Services list with brief descriptions
- [ ] Service area (cities or county)
- [ ] 8–15 project photos (the more real, the better)
- [ ] Before/after pairs if available
- [ ] License and insurance details (ISA cert, contractor license, etc.)
- [ ] Owner story / background
- [ ] 4–6 Google reviews
- [ ] Any awards, associations, or notable projects

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't make it look like a tech startup — this audience will bounce
- Don't use stock outdoor photography — the client's real work is always better
- Don't over-animate or add trendy effects — subtle and solid is right
- Don't skip the gallery — it's the most important section for project-based outdoor services
