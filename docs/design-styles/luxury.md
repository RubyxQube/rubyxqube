# The Luxury — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Wedding Planning · Luxury Real Estate · Catering · Day Spa · Jewelry · Event Design · Private Chef · Bespoke Services

---

## Core Philosophy

Scarcity and prestige. The client is self-selecting before they even reach out. This site should signal: "If you have to ask if you can afford us, you probably can't." Every detail is intentional — silence is as powerful as content. The site should feel like walking into a luxury boutique.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#0f0f0f`    | Near-black — rich and deep |
| Navbar     | `#0f0f0f`    | Seamless with body — sophisticated |
| Hero bg    | `#0f0f0f`    | Continuous canvas |
| Accent     | `#c9a84c`    | Antique gold — refined, never garish |
| Text       | `#fefce8`    | Warm cream — softer than pure white |
| Muted      | `#78716c`    | Dark warm gray |
| Cards      | `#1a1a1a`    | Barely lifted from black |

Client palette may use champagne and blush for weddings, ivory and black for high-end real estate, or deep jewel tones for spa. Keep the palette tight: dark background + one metallic accent + warm text only.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Medium-bold (600–700). Elegant, never aggressive. Less is more.
- **Hero H1:** 48–60px. One powerful phrase. "Your vision. Flawlessly executed." / "Boise's most sought-after wedding planner."
- **Body:** 16px, 1.9 line-height — extra spacious. Let the text breathe like a luxury editorial.
- **Consider:** A serif or high-contrast display font for headings (Playfair Display, Cormorant Garamond). Use sparingly.
- **Accent text:** Gold accent for one or two key words only. Not headlines, just the hook.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Black, seamless with page
- Logo center (or left, always refined)
- Minimal links: Work | Experience | Inquire
- No hamburger — use a clean slide-out or full-screen overlay menu
- CTA: "Inquire" — outline style, never a loud button

### 2. Hero Section
- Full-screen (100vh) if possible — makes a statement
- Hero image or looping video — the most beautiful image they have
- Minimal text overlay — business name, tagline, one link
- OR: Stark dark hero with just the headline and a thin gold underline
- Gold accent on 1–2 words only

### 3. Philosophy / About (Brief)
- 1 paragraph, maximum. Pull quote format.
- "We believe every event is a once-in-a-lifetime moment — and that every detail deserves to reflect that."
- No bullet lists in this section. Prose only.

### 4. Featured Work / Gallery
- The most important section for luxury clients — they're buying aesthetics
- Full-bleed grid or editorial masonry layout
- 6–12 images minimum, carefully curated
- Caption: project type + location (not client names)
- "View full portfolio →" or filtering by category (weddings / corporate / intimate)

### 5. Services / Experience
- NOT a grid of cards — too commercial
- Use a vertical list or tabbed layout:
  - "Full Service Planning" — 2–3 sentence description
  - "Day-Of Coordination" — 2–3 sentence description
  - "Design & Styling" — 2–3 sentence description
- Pricing is almost never listed — "By inquiry only" signals premium

### 6. Recognition / Press (if applicable)
- Featured in: logo bar of publications (The Knot, Boise Magazine, Idaho Statesman)
- Or awards and recognitions
- Doesn't need to be long — even one publication logo builds enormous credibility

### 7. Testimonials
- 2–3 testimonials, displayed large, in quotation marks
- Full name + event type. No photos needed — the quote stands alone.
- "Working with [Name] was the single best investment we made for our wedding."

### 8. Inquiry Form
- Not called "Contact" — it's "Work Together" or "Begin Your Journey" or "Inquire"
- Fields: Name, Email, Date (if applicable), Brief Description
- NO phone number field — luxury clients prefer written correspondence first
- CTA button: "Send Inquiry" or "Begin" — never "Submit"

### 9. Footer
- Ultra-minimal: logo, social links (Instagram especially), copyright
- Maybe: email address only (no phone on footer — calls are by appointment)

---

## Mobile Rules
- Full-screen hero scales gracefully — use `object-fit: cover` on images
- Gallery must be swipeable on mobile — use a grid or horizontal scroll
- Text stays legible — don't let luxury whitespace collapse to zero on mobile
- Inquiry form stays elegant — full-width, generous padding on inputs

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name, owner name, tagline
- [ ] 12–20 portfolio images (professionally shot — phone camera photos will not work here)
- [ ] Service list and descriptions (not prices)
- [ ] Philosophy / brand story (1–2 paragraphs, written in their voice)
- [ ] Press mentions or awards (if any)
- [ ] 2–3 client testimonials
- [ ] Preferred contact method (form only, email, DM)
- [ ] Instagram handle (luxury audiences live there)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't make it look busy — luxury is restraint; every section earns its place
- Don't use stock photography — no amount of CSS fixes a bad hero image
- Don't list pricing — inquiry-only is a positioning statement
- Don't use loud CTAs — "Book Now!" kills the vibe; use "Inquire" or "Begin"
- Don't add 6 nav links — luxury navigation is curated, not comprehensive
