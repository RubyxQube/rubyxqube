# The Wellness — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Chiropractic · Physical Therapy · Massage · Yoga · Nutrition · Mental Health · Acupuncture · Functional Medicine

---

## Core Philosophy

Calm confidence. The patient or client is already dealing with something uncomfortable — pain, stress, or a health concern. The site should immediately lower their anxiety and make them feel confident that they've found the right place. Clean, open, and reassuring.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#f0fdfa`    | Soft teal-white — fresh and calm |
| Navbar     | `#0d9488`    | Teal — grounded and health-forward |
| Hero bg    | `#ffffff`    | Clean white for the hero |
| Accent     | `#0d9488`    | Teal — CTAs, icons, highlights |
| Text       | `#134e4a`    | Dark teal-black — warm but professional |
| Muted      | `#94a3b8`    | Light gray for secondary text |
| Cards      | `#ccfbf1`    | Very light teal — gentle lift |

Client palette may use soft lavender (mental health), sage green (holistic), sky blue (sports PT), or warm white (yoga/pilates). Keep everything light and non-threatening.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Medium-bold (600–700). Calm and clear.
- **Hero H1:** 40–52px. Reassuring and benefit-focused. "Feel better. Move better. Live better." / "Boise's Trusted Chiropractor."
- **Body:** 16px, 1.8 line-height — extra generous for readability and a calm pace.
- **Avoid** heavy, aggressive typography. No all-caps section headers.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Teal or white background — both work. White feels more clinical/clean; teal feels more branded.
- Logo left, links: Services | About | Patient Info | Book
- Sticky on scroll — patients returning for the booking link need easy access
- CTA: "Book Appointment" or "Schedule a Visit" — teal button

### 2. Hero Section
- Clean white or teal-tinted background
- Professional but warm photo: practitioner with patient, or calming environment (not sterile/cold)
- **H1:** Benefit-first. What do they leave feeling?
- **Subhead:** Specialty + who they help. "Helping Boise athletes and desk workers live pain-free since 2015."
- **CTAs:** "Book Your First Visit" + "Learn What We Treat"
- Optional: "New patient special — $49 initial consultation" badge

### 3. Conditions / Who We Help
- "We help people with..." — list conditions or ideal patients
- Grid of conditions or a simple tag cloud
- Connects quickly with the visitor's situation

### 4. Services
- Cards per service: Chiropractic / Massage / Dry Needling / etc.
- Name + 2-sentence description + "Learn more →" or expand accordion
- Keep it patient-facing — "back pain relief" not "lumbar adjustment protocol"

### 5. How It Works / First Visit
- Patients are often anxious about trying a new practitioner
- 3-step visual process: 1: Book online → 2: Initial assessment → 3: Personalized care plan
- Reduces no-shows and first-visit anxiety

### 6. About / Meet the Team
- Very important for health/wellness — people need to trust their practitioner
- Photo + credentials + bio in friendly language
- "Dr. [Name] is a Boise native with 12 years in sports chiropractic..."
- Show certifications but explain what they mean: "CSCS — Certified Strength & Conditioning Specialist"

### 7. Testimonials / Results
- Patient success stories — with permission
- Before/after pain levels or function improvements (where appropriate)
- "I went from barely walking to running a half marathon after 8 sessions with Dr. [Name]."

### 8. Insurance / Patient Info
- "Do you take my insurance?" is the first question most patients have
- List accepted insurances or "We work with most major plans — call to verify"
- New patient forms link, HIPAA notice link

### 9. FAQ
- "What happens on my first visit?" / "Does it hurt?" / "How many sessions will I need?"
- Answer in plain language — demystifies the process

### 10. Booking / Contact CTA
- Embedded Cal.com or scheduling widget OR prominent "Call to Book" section
- Hours listed clearly
- Address + Google Maps embed

### 11. Footer
- Clean and light
- Practice name, practitioner name, credentials
- Phone, fax (if needed), address
- "Accepting new patients" if true

---

## Mobile Rules
- Booking CTA must be sticky or near-top on mobile — this is the conversion action
- Insurance info and hours should be easy to find on mobile
- Forms: new patient intake can be long — consider linking to a separate intake form page
- Provider headshots look great on mobile — don't crop or compress them

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Practice name, practitioner name(s), credentials
- [ ] Services/conditions treated (patient-facing language)
- [ ] Bio and headshot for each practitioner
- [ ] Accepted insurance list (or policy)
- [ ] Office hours and address
- [ ] New patient process ("what to expect")
- [ ] 3–5 patient testimonials (with permission)
- [ ] Booking method (phone, Cal.com, Jane App, etc.)
- [ ] New patient specials if any

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't make it feel cold and clinical — warmth and calm are the design goal
- Don't use medical stock photos (blue surgical gloves, stethoscopes) — they feel generic and scary
- Don't bury the booking CTA — it's the only goal of this site
- Don't use dense medical terminology in the hero — speak the patient's language
