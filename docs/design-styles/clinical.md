# The Clinical — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Dental · Medical Practice · Optometry · Dermatology · Urgent Care · Pediatrics · Veterinary · Pharmacy

---

## Core Philosophy

Immediate trust and clinical confidence. Patients are often anxious, in pain, or making a high-stakes health decision when they land on this site. They need to feel in good hands within 3 seconds. Clean, structured, and credentialed — this design removes doubt.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#f8fafc`    | Clinical near-white — clean and sterile |
| Navbar     | `#0369a1`    | Sky blue — medical authority |
| Hero bg    | `#ffffff`    | Pure white hero section |
| Accent     | `#0284c7`    | Bright sky blue — CTAs, icons |
| Text       | `#0c1a2e`    | Deep navy-black — authoritative |
| Muted      | `#94a3b8`    | Light blue-gray for secondary |
| Cards      | `#e0f2fe`    | Very light blue card lift |

Client palette may shift to deeper teal (holistic/integrative medicine), navy (dental specialty), or warm white + green (pediatrics/family practice). Keep clinical clarity as the goal — high contrast, nothing muddy.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Clear and confident (600–700). Professional and direct.
- **Hero H1:** 40–52px. Patient-first language. "Caring for [City] Families Since [Year]." / "Expert Dental Care — No Fear, No Wait."
- **Body:** 16px, 1.7 line-height. Clinical but approachable.
- **Credential text:** Use smaller, muted text for letters after names (DDS, MD, etc.) — don't bury them, don't make them the headline.
- **Avoid** script or display fonts — this is not the place for personality typography.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Blue background, white text
- Logo left (ideally a medical cross or professional mark)
- Links: Services | About | Patient Info | Book / Call
- Two CTAs: "Book Appointment" (primary) + "Patient Portal" (secondary outline)
- Phone number visible in the nav, tap-to-call on mobile

### 2. Urgency/Hours Top Bar (optional but effective)
- Thin bar above the main nav
- "Now Open — Mon–Fri 8am–5pm | Walk-ins Welcome | (208) xxx-xxxx"
- Background: accent blue or white with blue text

### 3. Hero Section
- Clean white background, professional photo: practitioner with patient (not stock)
- **H1:** Reassuring and confident
- **Subhead:** Specialty + location + what makes them different. 1–2 sentences.
- **CTAs:** "Book an Appointment" + "Call Us Now" — both prominent
- Badge/chip: "✓ Accepting New Patients" — extremely high conversion for medical sites
- Optional: "Most insurance accepted" in small text below CTAs

### 4. Trust / Credential Bar
- Horizontal row below hero
- Practice name | License number | Insurance logos | Years in business | Review count
- Or: credential badges — ADA Member | Delta Dental In-Network | HIPAA Compliant

### 5. Services / What We Treat
- Grid of service cards (3 columns desktop)
- Each: icon + service name + 1-sentence description + "Learn More →"
- Use patient language: "Tooth Pain Relief" not "Endodontic Therapy"
- Color: white cards on very light blue background (`#e0f2fe`)

### 6. Meet the Doctor(s)
- For medical practices, this is the most-read section
- Photo + full name + credentials + brief bio (4–5 sentences)
- Patients MUST feel like they know who's treating them before they book
- Multi-provider: 2–3 column grid of provider cards

### 7. New Patient / First Visit Info
- "What to expect on your first visit"
- Numbered steps: Check-in → Consultation → Treatment plan → Next steps
- "Bring your insurance card and a photo ID"
- New patient forms download link
- This section dramatically reduces no-shows

### 8. Insurance / Payment
- List accepted insurances (or "Most major insurances accepted — call to verify")
- Payment options: "We offer flexible payment plans"
- CareCredit or similar if applicable
- This is a top-3 question for every medical site — don't hide it

### 9. Reviews / Patient Testimonials
- 4–5 Google reviews with star ratings
- Full name + review text
- "See all [X] reviews on Google →" link
- Social proof is especially powerful when patients are anxious

### 10. FAQ
- "Do you take walk-ins?" / "How do I prepare for my appointment?" / "Do you treat children?"
- Accordion style, answered in plain language
- Anticipate anxiety — don't assume patients know how things work

### 11. Location / Hours / Contact
- Google Maps embed
- Clear hours table
- Multiple contact methods: phone, online booking, patient portal
- Address with navigation link for mobile

### 12. Footer
- Practice name, all provider names + credentials
- Address, phone, fax
- HIPAA / Privacy Policy link (required)
- "© [Year] [Practice Name] | Not a substitute for professional medical advice"

---

## Mobile Rules
- "Book Appointment" CTA must be immediately visible on mobile — no scrolling required
- Phone number must be tap-to-call everywhere
- Hours and address must be easy to find — they're the #1 mobile search goal
- Insurance info should load fast — this is a high-bounce page if slow

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Practice name, all provider names and credentials (DDS, MD, DO, etc.)
- [ ] Services list in patient-facing language
- [ ] Provider headshots (professional, white coat, welcoming expression)
- [ ] New patient process and required documents
- [ ] Insurance accepted list (or general policy)
- [ ] Office hours and address
- [ ] 4–6 Google reviews
- [ ] Online booking link (Zocdoc, Carestack, Dentrix, etc.) if they use one
- [ ] Patient portal URL
- [ ] FAQs in their own words

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use cold or sterile photography — medical ≠ unfriendly
- Don't bury "Accepting New Patients" — put it in the hero
- Don't use technical medical terms in patient-facing copy
- Don't skip the insurance section — it's a top reason people bounce
- Don't list only a form — patients want a phone number prominently displayed
