# The Builder — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** General Contractor · Home Builder · Residential Developer · Remodeling · Commercial Build-Out · Excavation · Civil Construction

---

## Core Philosophy

Projects speak louder than words. Builders win jobs on portfolio, reputation, and the impression that they can handle something big. This site needs to look serious, structured, and capable. Show the scale of work. Lead with proof, not promises.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#1c1c1c`    | Dark charcoal — industrial and serious |
| Navbar     | `#111111`    | Deeper black — anchors the top |
| Hero bg    | `#1c1c1c`    | Continuous dark canvas |
| Accent     | `#ea580c`    | Construction orange — high-visibility CTAs |
| Text       | `#ffffff`    | Full white on dark |
| Muted      | `#6b7280`    | Gray for secondary content |
| Cards      | `#2a2a2a`    | Lifted just above background |

Client palette may shift: navy + gold for luxury home builders, black + white for commercial fit-out, dark brown + orange for excavation/earthwork. Orange is the construction industry default — never use soft pastels.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Heavy (700–800). This is structural — the typography should feel like it has load-bearing capacity.
- **Hero H1:** 52–64px. Short and powerful. "Built Right. Built to Last." / "Treasure Valley's Most Trusted General Contractor."
- **Body:** 16–17px, clear and direct. No jargon — project descriptions should be readable to homeowners.
- **Label text:** Use uppercase labels for section headers (FEATURED PROJECTS / OUR SERVICES / ABOUT US) — works well with the industrial feel.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Dark background, white text, orange CTA button
- Logo left — ideally a badge-style or architectural mark
- Links: Projects | Services | About | Contact
- CTA: "Request a Bid" or "Start Your Project"
- Phone number visible on desktop

### 2. Hero Section
- Dark background with a full-bleed construction/project photo (aerial drone shot, completed home exterior, active jobsite)
- Bold overlay text — white headline, orange accent on key word
- **H1:** Confident and regional. "Boise's Premier Home Builder." or "We Build Idaho."
- **Subhead:** Scale and experience. "From custom homes to commercial build-outs — licensed, bonded, and on time."
- **CTAs:** "View Our Work" + "Request a Bid"
- Optional badge: "✓ Licensed & Bonded | ✓ X Years in Business | ✓ X Projects Completed"

### 3. Stats / By the Numbers
- High-impact row of statistics:
  - 🏠 [X] Homes Built | 📅 [X] Years Experience | ⭐ [X] 5-Star Reviews | 🔧 [X] Projects Completed
- Dark surface, orange number accent
- This section earns trust instantly at scroll depth 1

### 4. Featured Projects (Portfolio)
- THE most important section — builders live and die by their portfolio
- 6–12 projects minimum
- Each: project category (Custom Home / Kitchen Remodel / Commercial) + location + year
- Large images — full-width cards or 2-column masonry
- "View All Projects →" link to a full portfolio page (or filter by category)
- Before/after is gold for remodeling clients

### 5. Services
- Clean grid (3 columns)
- Each: service name + 2-line description + "Learn More →"
- Examples: New Home Construction | ADU / Guest House | Kitchen & Bath Remodel | Commercial Build-Out | Additions & Expansions
- Keep the list honest — only list what they actually do well

### 6. Process / How We Build
- Numbered steps: 1: Discovery & Bid → 2: Design Phase → 3: Permits & Scheduling → 4: Construction → 5: Final Walk & Handover
- This reduces anxiety for first-time homeowners or commercial clients who don't know the construction process
- 1–2 sentences per step max

### 7. About / Company Story
- 2-column: text left, crew/site photo right
- Year founded, owner's background, what makes them different
- License number, bonded status, insurance carrier (if willing to share)
- "Family-owned" / "Employee-owned" / "Not a franchise" — if true, say it

### 8. Testimonials
- 4–5 reviews from completed project clients
- Full name + project type is most credible: "James T. — Custom Home, Eagle, ID"
- Short and specific: "We moved in on time and on budget. That's rare."

### 9. Current/Active Projects (optional)
- "Currently building in: Meridian | Eagle | Nampa" — shows active business
- Links to project updates if available

### 10. CTA / Contact / Request a Bid
- "Ready to start your project?"
- Form: Name + Phone + Email + Project type (dropdown) + Brief description + Budget range (optional)
- "Or call us at (xxx) xxx-xxxx — we return all calls within 4 hours"

### 11. Footer
- Dark footer
- Business name, license number, insurance statement
- Serves: [City 1] · [City 2] · [City 3] · [County]
- "Licensed General Contractor — State of Idaho Lic. #XXXXXX"

## Mobile Rules
- Portfolio images must look great on mobile — full-bleed single column
- "Request a Bid" CTA must be tap-able immediately
- Stats row stacks to 2×2 grid on mobile
- Form stays clean — 5 fields max before "tell us more" textarea

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name, owner name, license number
- [ ] Years in business and project count
- [ ] 8–15 project photos with brief descriptions (category + location)
- [ ] Before/after pairs if they do remodeling
- [ ] Services list
- [ ] Construction process (how they work)
- [ ] 4–6 Google or project reviews
- [ ] Service area (cities or counties)
- [ ] Insurance / bonding status
- [ ] Preferred bid process (call, form, email)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't skip the portfolio — this is the #1 conversion driver for builders
- Don't use residential/warm colors — dark and industrial is right for this audience
- Don't write vague hero text like "Building Your Dreams" — be specific about who and where
- Don't hide the license number — it builds trust, especially for large contracts
- Don't make the form too long — "Name + Phone + Project Type" is enough to start a conversation
