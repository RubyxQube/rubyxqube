# The Professional — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Law · Accounting · Consulting · Financial Advisory · Insurance · Medical Offices · HR

---

## Core Philosophy

Credibility before everything. Clients in this market are making high-stakes decisions. The site must signal competence, stability, and trust within the first 5 seconds. Clean, structured, no gimmicks.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#f8fafc`    | Near-white — clean, clinical |
| Navbar     | `#1e3a5f`    | Deep navy — authority |
| Hero bg    | `#ffffff`    | Pure white hero |
| Accent     | `#1e3a5f`    | Navy — CTAs, borders, icons |
| Text       | `#1e293b`    | Near-black on light |
| Muted      | `#94a3b8`    | Secondary text |
| Cards      | `#f1f5f9`    | Subtle off-white lift |

Client colors may override — navy is default but charcoal, forest green, or burgundy all work for this style.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Medium-bold (600–700). Clean and structured, not aggressive.
- **Hero H1:** 40–52px. Clear value proposition. "Your [City] [Service] Attorney." "Trusted CPA for Small Business."
- **Body:** 16px, generous line-height (1.7). Reads like a professional document.
- **Don't** use ultra-heavy weights or condensed fonts. Professionalism = measured, not loud.

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Navy background, white text
- Logo left, nav links right, CTA button far right ("Schedule a Consultation")
- Optionally add a top bar: phone + email in small text above main nav (very professional look)

### 2. Hero Section
- White or very light background
- **2-column layout:** Text left (60%), professional photo right (40%)
- **H1:** Clear and direct. One sentence.
- **Subhead:** 2 sentences — what you do, who you serve.
- **CTAs:** Primary = "Schedule a Free Consultation" / Secondary = "Learn More"
- Optional: Credential logos — "Member of Idaho State Bar" / CPA badge / AICPA

### 3. Trust / Credentials Bar
- Horizontal row of trust signals
- Logo badges: associations, certifications, awards
- Numbers: "20+ years experience | 500+ clients served | $XM in assets managed"

### 4. Services Section
- Clean 3-column card grid
- Each card: service name, 2–3 sentence description, "Learn More →" link
- White cards on `#f1f5f9` background, navy left-border accent
- Optionally expand each card to a subpage

### 5. Why Choose Us / Differentiators
- 2-column: bullet list of differentiators left, team/office photo right
- Keep it factual: "Free initial consultation | Flat-fee billing available | Responsive — replies within 24 hours"
- Avoid superlatives without proof

### 6. Team Section (if applicable)
- Headshots + name + credentials + short bio
- Very important for law and consulting — clients buy the person, not the firm
- 2–3 column grid, clean cards

### 7. Testimonials
- 2–3 client quotes, prominent
- If possible: full name + company name (not just "John D.")
- Star rating optional for this style — feels more B2B than retail

### 8. FAQ Section (optional but effective for law/accounting)
- Accordion-style FAQ
- Answer the most common questions prospects have
- Reduces "tire-kicker" calls and builds confidence

### 9. Contact / CTA Section
- Clean form: Name, email, phone, message, "What do you need help with?" dropdown
- "Or call us directly: (xxx) xxx-xxxx"
- Office hours if relevant
- Office address + embedded map if walk-ins are welcome

### 10. Footer
- Two-column: nav links left, contact info right
- Disclaimer text if required (legal, financial)
- "© [Year] [Firm Name] | All Rights Reserved"

---

## Mobile Rules
- Stacked single column — no horizontal scroll
- CTA button always reachable (sticky header or near-top button)
- Team headshots resize gracefully — circular crop or card layout
- Forms must be fully usable on mobile

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Firm/business name + principals' names and credentials
- [ ] Practice areas or service lines
- [ ] "Why us" differentiators (years experience, specialties, awards)
- [ ] Professional headshots (essential for this style)
- [ ] 2–3 client testimonials with permission
- [ ] Association memberships or certifications
- [ ] Office address and hours
- [ ] Consultation policy (free, paid, phone vs. in-person)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use trendy design elements (gradients, bright colors, animations) — feels off-brand
- Don't list every service on the home page — link to a Services page
- Don't make the hero about you — make it about the client's problem
- Don't skip the team section — it's often the deciding factor for referral businesses
