# The Warm — Build Guidelines

> **Best for:** Cleaning · Childcare · Pet Services · Senior Care · Tutoring · Therapy · Dog Walking · House Sitting

---

## Core Philosophy

Trust through warmth. These businesses run on referrals and personal relationships. The site must feel safe, approachable, and real — like a neighbor recommending a friend. Reviews lead everything. Smiling real photos beat stock every time.

---

## Color System

| Role       | Default Value | Notes |
|------------|--------------|-------|
| Background | `#fffbeb`    | Warm cream — cozy and soft |
| Navbar     | `#92400e`    | Deep amber-brown — anchors the top |
| Hero bg    | `#fffbeb`    | Same as body |
| Accent     | `#d97706`    | Amber — warm, approachable CTAs |
| Text       | `#1c1917`    | Dark warm-black |
| Muted      | `#a8a29e`    | Warm gray for secondary |
| Cards      | `#fef3c7`    | Slightly warmer than body |

Client palette may lean sage green (childcare), lavender (therapy), or sky blue (pet services). Keep everything soft and non-threatening.

---

## Typography

- **Headings:** Friendly weight (600–700). Warm and human, not aggressive.
- **Hero H1:** 40–52px. Personal and reassuring. "We take care of what matters most." "Trusted by 200 Boise families."
- **Body:** 16px, 1.75 line-height — feels conversational and unhurried.
- **Consider a softer secondary font** for the business name or pull quotes — can use a rounded or humanist font. Nothing too formal.

---

## Layout Structure (Top to Bottom)

### 1. Navbar
- Warm brown or brand color background
- Logo left, minimal links (Services, About, Reviews, Contact)
- CTA: "Schedule a Free Consult" or "Book Now" — amber button

### 2. Hero Section
- Warm light background (or lifestyle image with warm tone)
- Photo: real photo of owner or team with clients — smiling, authentic
- **H1:** Reassuring and personal. Not sales-y.
- **Subhead:** 2 sentences. Who you serve and what you promise. "Caring, reliable [service] for [City] families. Background-checked, insured, and in love with what we do."
- **CTA:** "Meet [First Name]" or "Book a Free Consult" — less transactional than "GET A QUOTE NOW"

### 3. Social Proof — ABOVE THE FOLD (or just below hero)
- This is unusually high placement for reviews — intentional for this style
- 3 short quotes with ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐, customer name, "since [year]"
- "See 40+ reviews on Google →" link

### 4. How It Works / Process
- 3 simple numbered steps — reduces anxiety about trying a new service
- 1: "Tell us about your needs" → 2: "Meet [Name] / Get a free consult" → 3: "Sit back and relax"
- Icons, warm colors, no jargon

### 5. Services Section
- Clean cards or list — not a cold grid
- Each service: name + what's included + approximate pricing or "free consult to price"
- Warm-toned icon or illustration (not sharp, angular icons)

### 6. About / Meet the Owner
- This section is CRITICAL for personal service businesses
- Large owner photo, first-person voice: "Hi, I'm [Name]. I started [Business] because..."
- Credentials, insurance, background check mention
- Makes it personal — people hire people, not businesses, in this category

### 7. FAQ
- "Are you insured?" / "What happens if something breaks?" / "How do I book?"
- Anticipate fears and answer them honestly
- Accordion or simple Q&A list

### 8. Photo Gallery (optional)
- Real photos from jobs (with client permission)
- Childcare: kids activities · Cleaning: before/after · Pets: happy animals
- Instagram-style grid works well

### 9. CTA / Booking Section
- Warm, low-pressure: "Ready to get started? We'd love to meet you."
- Short form: Name + Phone + Service + Date/Time preference
- Or just a "Send us a message" form

### 10. Footer
- Warm and friendly — not corporate
- Business name, owner name, phone, email
- "Serving [City] & surrounding areas with care since [Year]"

---

## Mobile Rules
- Owner photo must look great on mobile — it's the most trust-building element
- Reviews section should be scrollable (horizontal scroll or stacked)
- Booking form must be simple and tappable — max 4 fields
- Phone must be tap-to-call

---

## Content to Collect from Client
- [ ] Business name, owner name, origin story (2–3 sentences)
- [ ] Services + pricing or pricing policy
- [ ] Photo of owner (professional-ish but real — not cheesy)
- [ ] 4–6 Google reviews
- [ ] Credentials, insurance, background check info
- [ ] Service area
- [ ] FAQ responses (their own words work great)
- [ ] Booking method (form, phone, online scheduler)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use corporate language — "providing exceptional solutions" kills trust here
- Don't use cold stock photos — no random smiling strangers; use real people
- Don't make the CTA aggressive — this audience responds to warmth, not urgency
- Don't skip the About section — it's doing 40% of the conversion work
