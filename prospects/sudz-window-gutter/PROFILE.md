## Recommended Aesthetic Direction for `/frontend-design`

**Suggested direction:** Clean residential — bright, airy, and refreshing. Think crisp whites, natural light, the feeling of a freshly cleaned window looking out onto a Boise morning. Not corporate-clean — warm and neighborhood-friendly. The brand should feel like the guy next door who happens to be really good at his job. Typography should be confident but approachable — a sturdy sans-serif for headlines, comfortable weight for body text. Photography-driven: big before/after, real windows, real homes.

**Why:** Chase's actual competitive advantage is that he's personal, shows up on time, and does excellent work. The design should amplify that — not try to make him look like a big company. Competitors (Squeegee Pros, Idaho Premier) lean corporate. Sudz can own the "trusted neighbor" positioning if the site reflects it visually.

**Accent color direction:** Keep Chase's existing brand colors (he wants to preserve color scheme and logo font — confirm exact colors from his current site). Likely sky blue or teal-adjacent given his logo. If there's flexibility: a clean sky blue with warm white backgrounds and a bright citrus or golden yellow as the action color — optimistic, clean, energizing. Avoid generic navy + green "contractor" palette.

**Note from Boyd:** Chase wants to keep the current color scheme and logo font. Pull exact hex values from sudzboise.com before designing. He is open to exploring other options beyond that, so present the "trusted neighbor" direction as an alternative or enhancement rather than a replacement.
